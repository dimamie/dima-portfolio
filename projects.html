Education platform redesign driven by measurable conversion paths

Educational content often assumes users will naturally move from reading to investing.

This redesign shifted the focus from passive article consumption to clearly defined conversion paths.

The redesign started with a workshop involving data analysts and conversion specialists.

Data showed that most traffic came from organic search, with users entering through specific articles rather than the main education hub — which shifted the focus toward optimizing individual article pages as primary entry points.

Instead of redesigning the hub top-down, the focus shifted to experimenting with ways to convert existing traffic.

This required stakeholder alignment and advocating for an approach driven by observed user behavior rather than default business assumptions.

The challenge was to balance maximum educational value with clear business objectives. Users arrived naturally through search engines to learn, but opening an account or downloading the app was not a natural next step.

Instead of forcing hard conversion, the flow capitalized on the learning intent. Asking for an email became a logical transition point — extending the educational journey while enabling a measurable, long-term conversion path.

On mobile, the conversion path opens in a bottom sheet, keeping the article visible in the background.

This preserves context, reduces friction, and allows users to return to reading without losing their place.

Since the article page became the main focus, engagement was increased through elements that supported the content instead of distracting from it.

An embedded instrument widget allowed users to view a live price chart with timeframe controls, connecting theory with real market data directly inside the article.

We strengthened the educational layer through short quizzes embedded in the article.

Beyond reinforcing knowledge, the quiz also acted as a soft conversion trigger — users with high scores were encouraged to open an account, linking demonstrated understanding with a natural next step.

AI-first help center and guided support

Users usually contact support by phone or email because those options are the easiest to notice.

The contact page was redesigned to prioritize AI chat as the primary support entry point, with common questions exposed as predefined prompts directly under the input.

Traditional contact channels like phone and email remain available, but are intentionally placed secondary to encourage faster, self-serve resolution.

After asking a question or selecting a predefined prompt, the user continues in a side chat.

This keeps context intact and allows the user to explore follow-up questions without starting over.

AI responses align with the user’s question while reinforcing key XTB value propositions, such as 0% commission on stocks and ETFs.

Answers include clear links to relevant pages, turning support conversations into guided conversion paths.

On mobile, the conversation opens in a full-screen overlay.

This optimizes limited screen space while keeping the chat easy to follow.

The conversation persists after the side chat is closed.

Users can return to it at any time via the button in the lower-right corner, without losing context.

Visualizing tax drag on long-term investing

Users often invest in regular brokerage accounts without realizing how much returns are lost to taxes over time.

This calculator shows the difference between investing in standard versus retirement accounts, making tax savings explicit and comparable.

It quantifies both the tax paid and the amount preserved through tax-advantaged investing, helping users understand the long-term impact before committing capital.

Most financial calculators are accurate but demand too much input, discouraging exploration.

This calculator minimizes required fields and uses a year-based segmented control to let users instantly see how starting earlier dramatically increases long-term returns.

Return estimates are often abstract or legally sensitive.

Basing calculations on the historical S&P 500 index grounds the results in reality while reducing legal risk compared to letting users define arbitrary future returns.

Financial instrument specification, discovery, and regulatory transparency

Users need to quickly verify whether a specific financial instrument is available before committing to the app, while regulators require full, standardized disclosure.

This public instrument specification presents complete legal data per instrument in a clear, scannable format that works for both pre-signup users and compliance needs.

Previously, users had to dig through multiple accordion-hidden tables to find a single instrument.

All instruments are now unified into one searchable table with tabs, making exploration and lookup significantly faster.

Wide data tables are difficult to use on mobile devices.

Mobile version restructures the table into instrument-level accordions, keeping navigation usable on small screens.

Standard industry rollover tables span multiple months and are hard to read at a glance.

A calendar-based monthly view makes upcoming rollovers easy to scan and reason about.

Advanced instrument concepts are often confusing and poorly explained, forcing users to leave the page to understand them.

Contextual tooltips provide clear, on-demand explanations without breaking the browsing flow.
